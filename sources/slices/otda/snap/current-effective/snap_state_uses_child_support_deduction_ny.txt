New York State Office of Temporary and Disability Assistance, SNAP Source Book,
Rev. 7/2025, Section 13: Determining Income, section G.2.b-c:

Both legally obligated and informal child support payments made by a SNAP
household member to non-household members are treated as income exclusions.
Local districts enter total gross income and child support paid, and ABEL
subtracts the child support payments from total household gross monthly income
before comparing income to the applicable gross and net income limits.

Model `snap_state_uses_child_support_deduction` as whether New York elects the
SNAP child-support deduction treatment under this current-effective state policy.
